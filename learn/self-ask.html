Self-Ask Prompting

AI asks and answers its own sub-questions to solve complex problems.

The Concept

Questions Lead to Answers

Self-Ask prompts the model to identify what sub-questions need answering before tackling the main question. The model explicitly asks "Follow-up question:" then answers it, building toward the final answer through intermediate steps.

Key Benefit

By making the questioning process explicit, Self-Ask produces more interpretable reasoning and catches missing information that would otherwise lead to errors.

The Pattern

"Are follow-up questions needed here?" triggers the model to decompose before answering.

Example

Self-Ask in Action

Question

"Who was president when the company that makes the iPhone was founded?"

Self-Ask Process

Follow-up question: What company makes the iPhone?
Answer: Apple makes the iPhone.

Follow-up question: When was Apple founded?
Answer: Apple was founded in 1976.

Follow-up question: Who was US president in 1976?
Answer: Gerald Ford was president in 1976.

Final answer: Gerald Ford was president when Apple was founded.
