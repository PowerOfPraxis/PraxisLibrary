Perspective Taking

SimToM

Simulate Theory of Mind through explicit perspective modeling.

The Concept

Modeling Mental States

SimToM (Simulating Theory of Mind) helps LLMs with tasks requiring understanding of what different people know or believe. It explicitly filters information to what each person could know, then reasons from that limited perspective.

Theory of Mind

The ability to attribute mental states—beliefs, intents, desires, knowledge—to oneself and others. LLMs often struggle with this because they see all information at once.

Two Steps

1) List only facts the target person knows. 2) Answer based solely on that filtered knowledge.

Example

SimToM in Action

Scenario

"Sally puts a ball in basket A. Sally leaves. Anne moves the ball to basket B. Where will Sally look for the ball?"

SimToM Approach

Step 1 - What Sally knows: Sally placed ball in basket A. Sally left before Anne moved it. Sally did NOT see the move.

Step 2 - Answer from Sally's view: Sally will look in basket A (where she believes the ball still is).

Applications

When SimToM Helps

False Belief Tasks

Questions about what someone believes when their belief differs from reality.

Social Reasoning

Predicting behavior based on limited information different people have.

Communication

Understanding what to explain based on what the audience already knows.
