Self-Refine

Iterative improvement through self-critique and refinement.

The Concept

Continuous Improvement Loop

Self-Refine leverages the model's ability to critique and improve its own outputs through iterative feedback cycles. Rather than accepting the first response, this approach treats generation as a starting point for refinement.

Core Insight

Language models can often identify problems in text they couldn't avoid generating in the first place. Self-Refine exploits this asymmetry between generation and evaluation capabilities.

Three Stages

1) Generate initial output. 2) Critique and identify weaknesses. 3) Refine based on feedback. Repeat as needed.

Process

The Three-Stage Cycle

1. Generate

Produce an initial response to the task. This first draft serves as raw material for refinement, not a final answer.

2. Feedback

Evaluate the output, identifying specific weaknesses, areas for improvement, and concrete suggestions for enhancement.

3. Refine

Generate an improved version that addresses identified issues while preserving existing strengths.

Example

Self-Refine in Action

Stage 1: Generate

"Write a product description for wireless earbuds."

Initial output: Basic description mentioning features...

Stage 2 & 3: Feedback + Refine

Feedback: "Review this description. What could be improved in terms of persuasiveness, clarity, and emotional appeal?"

Critique: Missing benefits, too technical, no emotional hook...

Refine: "Based on this feedback, rewrite with improvements incorporated."

Iteration Count

Research suggests 2-3 refinement cycles typically yield the best cost-to-quality ratio. Beyond this, improvements often become marginal.

Strategies

Feedback Approaches

Criteria-Based

Evaluate against explicit criteria like accuracy, clarity, and completeness with specific scores.

Role-Based

Critique from a specific perspective (skeptical customer, expert reviewer, target audience).

Error-Focused

Systematically identify factual errors, logical inconsistencies, and unsupported claims.
