Chain-of-Verification

Systematically verify and correct your own answers.

The Concept

Structured Self-Checking

Chain-of-Verification (CoVe) is a systematic approach to reducing hallucinations. After generating an initial response, the model creates specific verification questions, answers them independently, and then revises the original response based on any inconsistencies found.

Key Insight

Verification questions are answered independently from the original response, preventing the model from simply confirming its own assertions.

Four Steps

1) Generate baseline response. 2) Plan verification questions. 3) Execute verifications independently. 4) Generate final verified response.

Example

CoVe in Action

Baseline Response

"Name some politicians born in New York."
Initial: "Hillary Clinton, Donald Trump, Joe Biden..."

Verification Process

Verification Q1: Was Hillary Clinton born in NY? → No, Illinois

Verification Q2: Was Donald Trump born in NY? → Yes, Queens

Verification Q3: Was Joe Biden born in NY? → No, Pennsylvania

Final Response: Removes incorrect entries, keeps verified ones.

Verification Types

Execution Modes

Joint

Answer all verification questions together in one pass. Faster but may have cross-contamination.

2-Step

Generate questions first, then answer them in a separate step. Reduces bias from original response.

Factored

Answer each verification question completely independently. Most thorough but requires more computation.
