Information Theory

Max Mutual Information

Select examples that maximize shared information.

The Concept

Information-Theoretic Selection

Max Mutual Information (MMI) selects few-shot examples that maximize the mutual information between the prompt and the desired output. Examples are chosen based on how much they help the model predict the correct answer, not just surface similarity.

The Idea

Good demonstrations are those that make the correct output most predictable given the prompt—maximizing the information connection.

Practical Form

Often approximated by selecting examples where the model assigns highest probability to the correct answer.

Comparison

MMI vs Other Selection

Random

No optimization—examples picked arbitrarily from the pool.

Similarity

KNN-style—examples most similar to input in embedding space.

MMI

Examples that most increase probability of correct output.
