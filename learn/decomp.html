Problem Decomposition

Decomposed Prompting

Break complex tasks into specialized sub-procedures.

The Concept

Modular Problem Solving

Decomposed Prompting (DecomP) breaks complex tasks into smaller sub-tasks, each handled by a specialized "sub-procedure" or tool. Unlike simple decomposition, DecomP assigns different solving strategies to different sub-problems based on their nature.

Key Insight

Not all sub-problems need the same approach. Some need retrieval, others need calculation, and others need reasoning. DecomP matches the right tool to each sub-task.

The Framework

1) Decompose into sub-tasks. 2) Select appropriate handler for each. 3) Execute sub-procedures. 4) Integrate results.

Example

DecomP in Action

Complex Question

"What was the population of the city where Einstein was born when he won the Nobel Prize?"

Decomposed Approach

Sub-task 1 (Retrieval): Where was Einstein born? → Ulm, Germany

Sub-task 2 (Retrieval): When did Einstein win Nobel Prize? → 1921

Sub-task 3 (Retrieval): Population of Ulm in 1921? → ~57,000

Integration: Combine sub-answers into final response.

Components

Sub-Procedure Types

Retrieval

Look up facts, dates, or specific information from external sources or knowledge.

Calculation

Perform mathematical operations, comparisons, or logical computations.

Reasoning

Apply logical inference, causal analysis, or multi-step deduction.
