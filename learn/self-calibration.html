Self-Calibration

Know when to trust your answers.

The Concept

Assessing Confidence

Self-Calibration prompts the model to evaluate its own confidence in an answer. Well-calibrated confidence helps users know when to trust the response and when to seek verification or additional sources.

Why It Matters

Models can be confidently wrong. Self-calibration encourages honest uncertainty assessment rather than false confidence.

The Prompt

"After answering, rate your confidence (low/medium/high) and explain what makes you more or less certain."

Factors

Confidence Indicators

Knowledge Source

Is this from reliable training data, or is it extrapolation into uncertain territory?

Consistency

Would different reasoning paths lead to the same answer?

Completeness

Are there gaps in information that could change the answer?
