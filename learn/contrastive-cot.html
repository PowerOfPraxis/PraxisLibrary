Contrastive Chain-of-Thought

Learn what's right by seeing what's wrong.

The Concept

Good vs Bad Reasoning

Contrastive CoT provides examples of both correct AND incorrect reasoning chains. By showing common mistakes alongside proper solutions, the model learns to avoid reasoning pitfalls and understands why certain approaches fail.

The Power of Contrast

Showing only correct examples leaves ambiguity about what to avoid. Explicit counterexamples clarify boundaries and common errors.

Structure

Include "Correct reasoning: ..." followed by "Incorrect reasoning (common mistake): ..." in your examples.

Example

Contrastive CoT in Action

Correct Reasoning

"If a store has a 20% off sale and an item costs $50, the discount is $50 × 0.20 = $10, so the final price is $40."

Incorrect Reasoning (Common Mistake)

"If a store has a 20% off sale and an item costs $50, subtract 20 to get $30." ← This confuses percent with absolute value.
