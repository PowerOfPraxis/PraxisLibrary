Selection Strategy

Complexity-Based Prompting

Prefer longer, more detailed reasoning chains.

The Concept

More Steps, Better Answers

Complexity-Based Prompting samples multiple reasoning chains and selects based on complexity (number of steps) rather than just voting on answers. The insight is that more complex chains often indicate more thorough reasoning.

The Heuristic

Among chains that reach the same answer, prefer those with more intermediate steps. Very short chains may skip important reasoning.

Process

1) Generate multiple chains. 2) Count reasoning steps in each. 3) Weight votes by complexity. 4) Select the winner.

Comparison

vs Self-Consistency

Self-Consistency

Pure majority voting: every chain's answer counts equally regardless of how it got there.

Complexity-Based

Weighted voting: chains with more reasoning steps have higher influence on the final answer.
