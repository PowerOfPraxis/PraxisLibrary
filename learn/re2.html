RE2 (Re-Reading)

Read twice to understand better before responding.

The Concept

Simple but Effective

RE2 (Re-Reading) is a surprisingly simple technique: include the question twice in the prompt. This gives the model a second pass at comprehending the question, often catching nuances or details missed on the first read.

Why It Works

Like humans reading a complex question twice, re-reading allows the model to process information more thoroughly. The second pass reinforces understanding before generating an answer.

Implementation

Simply add "Read the question again: [question]" before asking for the answer.

Example

RE2 in Action

Standard Prompt

"A train leaves at 3pm traveling 60mph. Another train leaves the same station at 4pm traveling 80mph in the same direction. When does the second train catch up?"

RE2 Prompt

"A train leaves at 3pm traveling 60mph. Another train leaves the same station at 4pm traveling 80mph in the same direction. When does the second train catch up?

Read the question again: A train leaves at 3pm traveling 60mph. Another train leaves the same station at 4pm traveling 80mph in the same direction. When does the second train catch up?

Now solve this problem."

Best Uses

When RE2 Helps

Complex Questions

Questions with multiple conditions, constraints, or subtle requirements benefit from re-reading.

Long Contexts

When important details might be buried in lengthy text, re-reading highlights them.

Precise Tasks

Tasks requiring exact adherence to instructions, where missing a detail causes errors.
