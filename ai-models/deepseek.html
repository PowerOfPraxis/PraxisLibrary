About DeepSeek

DeepSeek™

A Chinese AI research lab producing open-source frontier models with novel training efficiency techniques, making high-quality AI accessible to the global research community.

Mission and Philosophy

DeepSeek is a Chinese AI research lab founded in 2023, known for releasing high-performance language models as open source with peer-reviewed arxiv papers documenting their methods. The lab has repeatedly demonstrated that frontier-class model quality is achievable at a fraction of the compute budget of Western competitors, reshaping industry assumptions about training cost.

Every major DeepSeek release comes with a technical report that other researchers can study, replicate, and build on. That transparency has made DeepSeek models a default starting point for open-source AI research and for teams that need to understand exactly how their models were trained.

The DeepSeek Lineup

DeepSeek segments its lineup around two primary model families, each optimized for a different class of workload:

  • DeepSeek-V3.2 is the current flagship, a general-purpose model designed for strong reasoning performance and available with open weights under permissive licensing.
  • DeepSeek-R1 is the dedicated reasoning model, purpose-built with extended chain-of-thought processing for math, science, and hard logical problems.
  • DeepSeek-V3 remains the widely-deployed production workhorse, the first model to demonstrate novel training efficiency techniques that dramatically cut compute costs without sacrificing quality.

Open Source and Deployment Posture

Every current DeepSeek model ships with open weights, downloadable from Hugging Face and deployable on any GPU infrastructure. That makes DeepSeek a strong fit for teams that need frontier-class quality without sending data to a hosted API, or that want to fine-tune a base model on proprietary domains.

DeepSeek also operates a hosted API for teams that prefer managed inference. The same model weights power both surfaces, so quality is consistent whether you self-host or use DeepSeek’s infrastructure.
