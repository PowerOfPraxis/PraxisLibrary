About Cohere

Cohere™

The Canadian AI company built for enterprise. Command models are purpose-tuned for RAG, tool use, and multilingual production workloads with a strong data sovereignty posture.

Mission and Philosophy

Cohere is a Canadian AI company founded in 2019 by former Google Brain researchers, including Aidan Gomez, a co-author of the original “Attention Is All You Need” Transformer paper. Based in Toronto, Cohere has built its product strategy around enterprise customers from day one, emphasizing retrieval augmented generation, strong multilingual support, and deployment flexibility.

Where other frontier labs chase consumer chat and general benchmarks, Cohere deliberately tunes its models for the workloads enterprises actually run: grounded Q&A over proprietary documents, multi-step tool use, multilingual content, and agentic workflows with auditable behavior.

The Command A Family

Cohere’s current flagship lineup centers on the Command A model, with specialized variants for vision and reasoning:

  • Command A is the flagship, a 111B parameter Mixture-of-Experts purpose-built for enterprise RAG and agentic workflows, supporting 23 languages with a 256K context window.
  • Command A Vision extends the flagship with multimodal image understanding, a 112B open-weights model for enterprise document and visual content processing.
  • Command A Reasoning is the hybrid reasoning variant for hard agentic tasks, 111B parameters with the same 256K context window as Command A.

Deployment Flexibility and Enterprise Posture

Every Command A variant ships with open weights, giving enterprises a choice between the hosted Cohere API, cloud marketplace deployments on Oracle OCI, AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud, or fully self-hosted inference on their own infrastructure. That flexibility is rare among frontier labs and particularly valuable for regulated industries.

Cohere has built its enterprise compliance story around data sovereignty, GDPR, HIPAA-eligible deployments through cloud partners, and contractual commitments that customer data is not used to train future models. For teams with strict procurement requirements, Cohere is often the easiest frontier lab to get through legal review.
