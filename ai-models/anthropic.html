About Anthropic

Anthropic™

An AI safety company building reliable, interpretable frontier models through Constitutional AI.

Mission and Philosophy

Founded in 2021 by Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, and former members of OpenAI, Anthropic is built around AI safety research and interpretability. The company pioneered Constitutional AI (CAI), a training technique where models are guided by a written set of principles rather than relying solely on human feedback loops.

This philosophy shapes the character of every Claude release, producing a model family consistently rated among the most thoughtful and instruction-faithful frontier models on the market.

The Claude Tier Strategy

Anthropic segments its lineup into three named tiers, each built to be the right default for a specific class of workload:

  • Opus is the flagship, built for the hardest reasoning and the longest, most complex agentic work.
  • Sonnet is the balanced workhorse for production workloads that need consistent quality at scale.
  • Haiku is the speed-and-cost specialist for high-throughput, low-latency applications.

Enterprise Posture

Anthropic has built a reputation for policy clarity. API inputs and outputs are not used to train future Claude models by default, and business tiers add options for zero data retention, HIPAA-eligible Business Associate Agreements, and regional availability across Anthropic directly, Amazon Bedrock, and Google Cloud Vertex AI.

For teams whose procurement and compliance teams care as much as their engineers do, that governance posture has been a meaningful part of why Claude shows up in regulated industries.

Best Use Cases

Deep Research and Synthesis

Ingest entire document libraries, research papers, and multi-report corpora in a single call. The 1M context window lets Opus produce structured summaries with cross-document references and grounded citations.

Agentic Software Engineering

Multi-file refactors, codebase-aware reviews, and long debugging sessions that require the model to hold an entire repository in context while reasoning about architecture and edge cases.

Long-Document Legal and Clinical Work

Contracts, legal discovery bundles, multi-hour meeting transcripts, and clinical note review. Most documents fit into a single request without chunking or retrieval plumbing.

Strategic Analysis and Planning

Board memos, competitive analyses, and multi-stakeholder decision support where the model needs to weigh nuanced trade-offs and justify recommendations with explicit reasoning.

Complex Tool Orchestration

Multi-step autonomous workflows where the model calls several APIs, reasons about the results, and adjusts its plan as it goes. Extended thinking plus parallel tool calls make this the flagship use case.

What Opus 4.6 Excels At

  • Depth of reasoning. Extended thinking lets Opus 4.6 work through multi-step problems in a structured internal monologue before responding, producing answers that consistently catch edge cases a direct-response model would miss.

  • Instruction faithfulness. Constitutional AI training produces a model that follows complex layered instructions closely, makes its reasoning legible when asked, and surfaces thoughtful alternatives when it identifies a better path.

  • Long-context fidelity. The 1M token window is not just large, it is usable. Opus 4.6 maintains comprehension and recall across the full window, making whole-document workflows practical rather than aspirational.

What Sonnet 4.5 Excels At

  • Balance. Sonnet 4.5 delivers the majority of Opus-class quality at a fraction of the latency and cost, making it the pragmatic default for production systems that need consistent output across thousands or millions of calls.

  • Responsiveness. Median latency is well-suited to interactive experiences like chat, IDE completions, and agent loops, while the opt-in extended thinking mode is there when a request genuinely needs deeper deliberation.

  • Throughput economics. Combined with prompt caching and the Batch API, Sonnet 4.5 is frequently the most cost-effective path to production-grade AI for teams running large-volume workloads.

What Haiku 3.5 Excels At

  • Speed. Haiku 3.5 responds in a fraction of the time the larger Claude tiers need, making it the right default for any workload where response time is a primary constraint.

  • Cost efficiency. Per-token pricing on Haiku is the lowest in the Claude family, and when combined with prompt caching and the Batch API, it makes AI features viable in workloads that would otherwise be uneconomical.

  • Capability per dollar. Despite being the fastest and least expensive tier, Haiku 3.5 carries a full 200K context window, vision input, and tool use, capabilities that only a generation ago were flagship-exclusive.
